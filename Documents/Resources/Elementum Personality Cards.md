# Part One · Ten Stems 干

Each stem is a person's fundamental way of being in the world — not a mood or a choice, but the engine underneath everything else. These profiles describe both the interior experience (who this person is) and the external energy force (what this element does when it enters a chart or a moment).

## 甲 Yang Wood — The Oak
*Forward motion as structure, not ambition · The Growth Impulse (Yang)*

**Keywords:** Visionary · Initiating · Growth-driven · Integrity-bound · Consolidation-resistant

### Psychological Core
**The Perpetual Initiator**

He is the person who starts things that outlast him — driven by a forward momentum so structural it doesn't register as ambition. Sees what could be before others see what is.

### Behavioral Profile

**How they experience the world**
The Oak doesn't decide to grow toward things. It just grows, the way a tree grows toward light — not from ambition but from structural necessity. There is a forward orientation in this person so fundamental it doesn't register as drive. It registers as the only natural direction.

**What they're genuinely good at**
Seeing what something could become before anyone else does. Starting things — not because they are asked to but because the thing needs to exist. Providing direction without requiring acknowledgment for having provided it.

**Where they consistently get stuck**
The reaching outruns the roots. They commit to something genuinely and deeply — then see the next thing before the current thing has fully landed. The Oak that grows tallest is the one whose roots went down first. This is the work they resist.

**What changes when conditions are right**
The classical principle: raw wood becomes useful timber only when something shapes it. When this person finds the right structure — the right cause, the right collaborator, the right adversity — the growth stops being scattered and starts being directed. Everything changes in the right soil.

**What they rarely admit**
That they often don't know what they're building toward — only that they're building. The forward motion is real. The destination is often invented after arrival.

### Psychology & Archetype Resonances

| Dimension | Profile |
|---|---|
| Big Five | High Openness/Intellect · High Assertiveness · variable Industriousness (challenge is consolidation, not initiation) |
| Jungian Function | Extraverted Intuition (Ne) + Extraverted Thinking (Te) — generates possibilities and moves toward them structurally |
| Attachment Style | Secure-leaning with over-extension risk — commits deeply then outgrows before fully landing |
| Shadow Mechanism | Integrity vs. Rigidity — would rather break than bend. Authority is tolerated only when earned through demonstrated quality. |

**Archetype resonances:** ENTP / ENTJ (MBTI) · The Pioneer (Brand) · The Hero (Jungian) · The Magician (Campbell) · Type 7 / Type 3 (Enneagram) · Aries / Sagittarius (Western) · Manifesting Generator (HD)

### As an External Energy

**Keywords:** Initiation · Rising ambition · New beginnings · Competition · Upward thrust

**What is this energy?**
甲 is the first stem — the energy of spring's first upward break through frozen ground. Classically described as 木之阳, it represents a forceful, non-negotiable impulse to rise and expand. It is the atmosphere of "things beginning whether you're ready or not."

**What does it represent in real life?**
New projects launching all around you. Ambition filling the environment. People becoming more assertive, initiating more, competing more openly. The landscape gets crowded — more people reaching for the same things at the same time.

**When you encounter it in 流年大运**
When 甲 energy enters a luck cycle or annual pillar, it activates everything dormant. The environment rewards boldness and punishes hesitation. Career opportunities emerge suddenly. Competitors become more visible. For charts that benefit from Wood: forward momentum. For already Wood-heavy charts: restlessness, overcommitment, difficulty consolidating.

### Energy Manual

**When concentrated / overpowering**
New ideas launch before old ones land. Commitments pile up faster than they can be honored. The chart or period is saturated with initiating energy — the classical principle warns of "growth without harvest." The specific trap: more beginnings accumulate than can ever be finished.

**When open / receptive or missing**
Initiative is genuinely harder to find. The environment or person waits for external permission before moving. Starting things feels risky rather than natural. Projects that need a champion stall at the beginning.

**How to seek and use it as a catalyst**
Enter it by initiating the one specific thing you have been circling — not ten. Commit to a direction and enter it fully before the window moves. Volunteer for the visible role, launch the project, make the first move. Outcome: things that had no momentum suddenly have it.

**How to release and channel it as resistance**
When 甲 energy creates friction — growth impulse is competing with structure rather than feeding it. Redirect it: use the Metal quality (pruning, cutting options) to define which growth is worth nurturing. Stop adding beginnings — close enough open loops to make space for one thing to land. Outcome: restlessness converts into directed momentum.

## 乙 Yin Wood — The Vine
*Navigation as intelligence, not accommodation · The Growth Impulse (Yin)*

**Keywords:** Adaptive · Strategically perceptive · Resilient · Destination-fixed · Coiling intelligence

### Psychological Core
**The Intelligent Navigator**

She survives and arrives by reading surfaces others don't notice, coiling around what resists, finding the route that wasn't obvious. The destination is fixed. The path is infinitely flexible.

### Behavioral Profile

**How they experience the world**
The Vine knows where it's going before it knows how to get there. It reads surfaces, finds routes others miss, works around what resists rather than breaking through it. The intelligence is in the navigation, not the force.

**What they're genuinely good at**
Finding the route that wasn't obvious. Arriving where they intended by means that confuse people who expected force. Reading the room before anyone else has registered there is a room to read.

**Where they consistently get stuck**
The adaptability can become the whole point. When every surface is interesting and every route is possible, the destination can blur. The vine that accommodates everything eventually loses the thread of where it was going.

**What changes when conditions are right**
The Vine's gifts fully activate when the environment is genuinely worth the full reach — a surface that deserves the climbing. There is also a specific condition it cannot navigate around: environments that are genuinely cold or aggressively cutting don't challenge the flexibility — they damage it. The adaptive intelligence requires warmth to function at all.

**What they rarely admit**
How exhausting it is to always be the one who navigates. The flexibility is not optional — it is structural. But it costs something real, and that cost goes unnamed.

### Psychology & Archetype Resonances

| Dimension | Profile |
|---|---|
| Big Five | High Agreeableness/Politeness · High Openness · moderate Conscientiousness — navigation as primary mode |
| Jungian Function | Extraverted Feeling (Fe) + Introverted Intuition (Ni) — reads the environment and moves toward a fixed interior destination |
| Attachment Style | Anxious-preoccupied lean — hyper-attuned to relational signals, high accommodation activation |
| Shadow Mechanism | Accommodation vs. Drift — the flexibility that enables navigation can gradually dissolve the destination it was serving |

**Archetype resonances:** ENFJ / ESFJ (MBTI) · The Hero (Brand) · The Great Mother / Anima (Jungian) · Type 2 / Type 7 (Enneagram) · Leo / Aries (Western) · Manifestor (HD) · The Lover (Campbell)

### As an External Energy

**Keywords:** Networking · Adaptive intelligence · Relationship weaving · Soft persistence · Finding the gap

**What is this energy?**
乙 is the energy of the vine after the tree — not the initial thrust but the intelligent navigation that follows. Where 甲 forces upward, 乙 feels its way forward. It is the atmosphere of "the smart path matters more than the direct one."

**What does it represent in real life?**
Relationship dynamics becoming more important than raw capability. Opportunities arriving through connections rather than competition. The environment rewards those who read the room well and adapt quickly.

**When you encounter it in 流年大运**
When 乙 energy enters a luck cycle or annual pillar, the environment shifts toward relationship-intelligence. Raw power becomes less effective. Opportunities arrive through networking and referrals. For charts that benefit from Wood: collaborative momentum. For already Wood-heavy charts: over-accommodation, difficulty committing.

### Energy Manual

**When concentrated / overpowering**
Everything accommodates, nothing commits. The chart or period is saturated with relational intelligence — socially exhausting because the room-reading never stops. The classical trap: 柔弱 (too yielding) — the vine that never finds a surface firm enough to climb.

**When open / receptive or missing**
Relational intelligence gaps appear. Only direct approaches are available, which fail in complex political terrain. Opportunities requiring connection rather than competence get missed. Relationships become transactional.

**How to seek and use it as a catalyst**
Engage it through the relational field: network, collaborate, let others open the door. Enter the room where the right connections already exist. Allow others to facilitate. Outcome: networks activate, referrals arrive, opportunities appear through relationship.

**How to release and channel it as resistance**
When 乙 energy creates friction — flexibility is working against your actual goal. You've accommodated yourself out of your original position. Fix: name where you're going before entering adaptive mode. Know your destination before navigating. Outcome: adaptability becomes intelligent navigation rather than drift.

## 丙 Yang Fire — The Sun
*Warmth as structural property, not personality trait · The Visibility Impulse (Yang)*

**Keywords:** Radiant · Generous · Presence-generating · Unmodulated · Warmth-structural

### Psychological Core
**The Involuntary Illuminator**

He doesn't choose to warm the room. The room warms because he's in it. The light arrives before the decision to share it.

### Behavioral Profile

**How they experience the world**
The warmth isn't performed — it's structural. People gather around this person the way people gather around genuine warmth. The Sun doesn't experience itself as particularly generous. It just — is.

**What they're genuinely good at**
Genuine presence that creates trust before it's been earned. The capacity to inspire not through rhetoric but through real conviction — which works in rooms where rhetoric fails. Warmth that persists across seasons.

**Where they consistently get stuck**
The light is not a volume knob. It's very difficult to be invisible when you need to be. The same openness that makes you extraordinary makes privacy genuinely hard to maintain.

**What changes when conditions are right**
The Sun needs fuel that sustains it, not just targets that absorb it. Meaningful work, meaningful causes, genuine relationships that give back — these aren't optional. Without them, the broadcast continues but the source depletes. And there is a second condition: the warmth is most powerful when there is something to direct it toward.

**What they rarely admit**
That the warmth costs something. From the outside it looks inexhaustible. From the inside, there are seasons when the sky is overcast and the light still has to come anyway.

### Psychology & Archetype Resonances

| Dimension | Profile |
|---|---|
| Big Five | High Extraversion/Warmth · High Agreeableness/Compassion · variable Conscientiousness — broadcasting as default mode |
| Jungian Function | Extraverted Feeling (Fe) as primary function — warmth and social orientation as structural rather than strategic |
| Attachment Style | Secure-leaning with over-extension risk — gives freely, sometimes past the point of reciprocity |
| Shadow Mechanism | Warmth vs. Diffusion — the same radiance that draws people in can prevent the depth that proximity deserves |

**Archetype resonances:** ENFJ / ESFP (MBTI) · The Ruler / Entertainer (Brand) · The Self / Hero (Jungian) · The Lover (Campbell) · Type 2 / Type 7 (Enneagram) · Leo / Sagittarius (Western) · Manifestor (HD)

### As an External Energy

**Keywords:** Visibility · Public exposure · Recognition · Radiance · Everything in the light

**What is this energy?**
丙 is the sun — the great broadcasting fire that lights everything simultaneously. Classically described as 火之阳, it is outward, radiant, and indiscriminate. It is the atmosphere of "nothing stays hidden; everything becomes more visible."

**What does it represent in real life?**
Public recognition opportunities. Career visibility increasing. Social environments becoming more energized. What has been built becomes more publicly known. Equally, what has been concealed becomes harder to keep covered.

**When you encounter it in 流年大运**
When 丙 energy enters a luck cycle or annual pillar, visibility increases across the board. Career recognition arrives quickly. The shadow is equally real: 丙 reveals everything. For charts that benefit from Fire: activating recognition period. For already Fire-heavy charts: burnout, overexposure, reputation risk.

### Energy Manual

**When concentrated / overpowering**
Everything is lit up — and nothing remains private. Excess 丙 is literal overexposure. The classical warning: 火炎土燥 — when Fire becomes excessive, the ground beneath it dries and cracks. High-energy but unstable, brilliant but exhausting.

**When open / receptive or missing**
Visibility is genuinely harder to access. Quality work exists but doesn't surface. The classical image: a lantern inside a jar — the flame is real but the light doesn't reach others.

**How to seek and use it as a catalyst**
Activate it by entering public or visible contexts: put the work in front of others, attend the gathering, publish, take the speaking slot. This produces momentum specifically through being seen. Outcome: recognition accelerates. Career momentum unsticks.

**How to release and channel it as resistance**
When 丙 energy creates friction — warmth and visibility are running ahead of what the chart can sustain. Direct the exposure strategically rather than broadcasting broadly. Use Water quality (depth, selectivity) to give form to what's shown. Outcome: overexposure converts into targeted presence.

## 丁 Yin Fire — The Candle
*Complete illumination of exactly what it's pointed at · The Visibility Impulse (Yin)*

**Keywords:** Precise · Focused · Depth-seeking · Selective · Inner-lit

### Psychological Core
**The Focused Witness**

She illuminates completely — but only what she's pointed at. The quality of attention is extraordinary. The range is chosen, not broad.

### Behavioral Profile

**How they experience the world**
This person doesn't warm a room the way the Sun does. They illuminate a specific person, problem, or question fully — the depth of their attention changes what it rests on. People feel genuinely seen in their presence, not generally warmed.

**What they're genuinely good at**
The quality of attention that sees what others in the room missed. Making people feel genuinely known rather than generally appreciated. The precision of focus that, once directed, is transformative.

**Where they consistently get stuck**
The precision can turn on itself. The Candle examining everything eventually examines what remains unexamined — which is the Candle itself. Overthinking, over-refining, the standard applied past the point of productive return.

**What changes when conditions are right**
The Candle works best when its autonomy to direct the light is genuinely its own. The classical insight that changes things: the Candle with the right material to work on doesn't just illuminate — it forges. There is a transformation available when the right fuel is present: not the Candle that shows you what something is, but the Candle that changes what it examines.

**What they rarely admit**
That the selectivity is partly protection. The range of the Candle's light is chosen. Not everything that isn't illuminated has been missed.

### Psychology & Archetype Resonances

| Dimension | Profile |
|---|---|
| Big Five | High Openness/Aesthetics · High Conscientiousness/Orderliness · moderate Neuroticism — precision and depth as primary orientation |
| Jungian Function | Introverted Intuition (Ni) + Introverted Feeling (Fi) — perceives deeply into what is attended to; cares specifically |
| Attachment Style | Fearful-avoidant lean — high capacity for depth, high threshold for entry; the selectivity is partly the defense |
| Shadow Mechanism | Precision vs. Paralysis — the same discriminating intelligence that enables depth can prevent completion |

**Archetype resonances:** INFJ / INTJ (MBTI) · The Sage / Creator (Brand) · The Wise Old Man / Anima (Jungian) · The Shape-Shifter (Campbell) · Type 4 / Type 1 (Enneagram) · Scorpio / Virgo (Western) · Projector (HD)

### As an External Energy

**Keywords:** Focused insight · Careful examination · Refinement · Hidden details · Precision light

**What is this energy?**
丁 is the candle or forge fire — concentrated, intentional, illuminating exactly what it points at. Where 丙 lights everything, 丁 lights one thing completely. It is the atmosphere of "the details that were missed before now come into focus."

**What does it represent in real life?**
Learning and skill refinement becoming more productive than broad expansion. The fine print in agreements becoming relevant. Relationships and projects kept at surface level getting examined more closely.

**When you encounter it in 流年大运**
When 丁 energy enters a luck cycle or annual pillar, the environment rewards attention to detail over broad ambition. Agreements should be read closely. For charts that benefit from Fire: focused development and meaningful insight. For already Fire-heavy charts: overthinking, excessive self-examination, anxiety.

### Energy Manual

**When concentrated / overpowering**
The examining quality becomes exhaustive. Every detail gets scrutinized, every relationship held to a standard of depth it may not be designed for. The classical problem: excess 丁 burns away what it examines — the candle that runs out of its own fuel.

**When open / receptive or missing**
Things stay at surface level. Work is produced but not polished. Relationships remain pleasant but never reach real depth. There is energy but no concentrated directional light.

**How to seek and use it as a catalyst**
Activate it by going deeper into one thing rather than broader across many. Study the subject fully. Have the one-on-one conversation. Revise the work one more time past where it felt done. Outcome: depth produces results that breadth never could.

**How to release and channel it as resistance**
When 丁 energy creates friction — precision is examining rather than building. Set a completion point before beginning — decide what "done" looks like so the refining faculty has a finish line. Outcome: precision lands constructively rather than circling endlessly.

## 戊 Yang Earth — The Mountain
*The ground others orient by without naming · The Stability Impulse (Yang)*

**Keywords:** Stable · Load-bearing · Patient · Orienting · Unmoved

### Psychological Core
**The Unnamed Foundation**

He is the person others build on — not because he positioned himself there, but because the ground was solid and the building began. Stability as structure, not strategy.

### Behavioral Profile

**How they experience the world**
This person doesn't experience themselves as particularly stable — they experience themselves as simply being here, being present, being the same person they were yesterday. Others experience the constancy as something extraordinary.

**What they're genuinely good at**
Sustained reliability that isn't effort — it's constitution. Patience with process that allows completion of what others give up on. A groundedness so consistent it becomes a fact others orient by.

**Where they consistently get stuck**
The same solidity that makes this person irreplaceable makes necessary change genuinely difficult. The Mountain's recurring challenge: distinguishing between holding ground and refusing to move.

**What changes when conditions are right**
The Mountain activates fully when it has genuine weight to hold — a cause, an institution, a relationship that actually deserves the stability. Without real material, the groundedness becomes heaviness.

**What they rarely admit**
That the stability is sometimes loneliness. The Mountain is always where people left it. It doesn't move toward what it values. It hopes what it values comes back.

### Psychology & Archetype Resonances

| Dimension | Profile |
|---|---|
| Big Five | High Conscientiousness/Industriousness · moderate-high Agreeableness · low Neuroticism — stability and reliability as primary orientation |
| Jungian Function | Introverted Sensation (Si) as primary function — anchored in concrete reality, reliable internal reference standard |
| Attachment Style | Secure base — provides stability to others; own attachment needs often understated |
| Shadow Mechanism | Stability vs. Rigidity — the Mountain that refuses to allow geological change eventually creates the pressure that causes the earthquake |

**Archetype resonances:** ISTJ / ISFJ (MBTI) · The Caregiver / Ruler (Brand) · The Great Father / Self (Jungian) · The Mentor (Campbell) · Type 1 / Type 6 (Enneagram) · Taurus / Capricorn (Western) · Generator (HD)

### As an External Energy

**Keywords:** Consolidation · Foundation · Stabilizing force · Resistance to change · Accumulation

**What is this energy?**
戊 is the mountain or great plain — immovable, orienting, the accumulating ground. Classically described as 土之阳, the earth that "stands without moving." It is the atmosphere of "things solidify and slow; what is built now is built to last."

**What does it represent in real life?**
Real estate and property becoming more prominent. Consolidation of resources and positions. The environment favoring stability and reliability over speed and novelty. Things taking longer to move.

**When you encounter it in 流年大运**
When 戊 energy enters a luck cycle or annual pillar, the environment shifts toward consolidation. Property matters become significant. The pace of change slows. Reliability becomes more valuable than innovation. For charts that benefit from Earth: grounding and accumulation. For already Earth-heavy charts: stagnation, difficulty changing course.

### Energy Manual

**When concentrated / overpowering**
The grounding quality becomes literal heaviness. Classical texts warn 土重则滞 (excess Earth creates stagnation) — when the Mountain grows too dense, nothing moves across it. Opportunities pass because the inertia of consolidation prevents engagement.

**When open / receptive or missing**
Foundational stability is hard to access. Things don't land or hold. Projects start but don't root. Financial and physical accumulation is more difficult. The environment feels unstable in ways that are exhausting rather than exciting.

**How to seek and use it as a catalyst**
Activate it by doing the foundation work: establish the structure, formalize the arrangement, secure the position, sign the commitment. The productive use: make the tentative fixed. Outcome: what was provisional becomes stable. Resources stop moving and start accumulating.

**How to release and channel it as resistance**
When 戊 energy creates friction — stability is working against necessary movement. Don't try to remove the Mountain; find the path it allows. Use Wood energy (growth impulse, directionality) to create movement within the structure. Outcome: resistance becomes framework.

## 己 Yin Earth — The Field
*Growing things in others without announcing it · The Stability Impulse (Yin)*

**Keywords:** Nourishing · Receptive · Patient · Cultivating · Quietly fertile

### Psychological Core
**The Silent Cultivator**

She grows things in others — people, projects, ideas — without announcing the growing. The fertility is structural. She may not recognize how much has taken root.

### Behavioral Profile

**How they experience the world**
This person doesn't experience themselves as particularly nurturing — they experience themselves as paying attention, as caring about what actually happens to the things and people they're involved with. Others experience it as being genuinely cultivated.

Sensitivity to what things need in order to grow. Patience with the actual pace of development. A quality of sustained care that is consistent across seasons — not dependent on being thanked.

**Where they consistently get stuck**

Two specific conditions the Field needs: warmth (genuine appreciation and reciprocal care that keeps the soil from going cold) and something that works the soil — directional challenge that activates the cultivating capacity rather than simply extracting from it. A field only harvested loses fertility.

**What they rarely admit**
The resentment. The Field has been producing for a long time, and the accounting has not been balanced. This is not said, because the Field doesn't keep accounts. But it is felt.

**What does it represent in real life?**
Health and wellbeing becoming more prominent. Long-term investments becoming more productive. The environment rewarding patience over aggressive acquisition. Relationships deepening rather than expanding.

**When you encounter it in 流年大运**
When 己 energy enters a luck cycle or annual pillar, the environment supports patient cultivation. Health matters tend to surface. Long-term investments begin paying quiet dividends. For charts that benefit from Earth: genuine nourishment. For already Earth-heavy charts: overthinking, accumulation of worry, absorbing others' difficulties.

### Energy Manual

**When concentrated / overpowering**
The receptive quality becomes absorptive without filtration. Classical texts describe 己土混浊 — when the fertile soil absorbs too much, it loses its cultivating quality and becomes mud. Overthinking, rumination, and carrying others' emotional weight characterizes the excess state.

**How to release and channel it as resistance**
When 己 energy creates friction — the absorptive quality is pulling in more than can be processed. Choose explicitly what deserves the cultivation energy. Use Metal energy (boundary, definition) to create filtration. Outcome: caregiving becomes targeted and powerful rather than diffuse and depleting.

## 辛 Yin Metal — The Jewel
*Quality perceived as temperature — before the question forms · The Definition Impulse (Yin)*

**Keywords:** Discerning · Refined · Standard-holding · Aesthetically precise · Selective

### Psychological Core
**The Perceptual Perfectionist**

She perceives quality and its absence the way others perceive temperature — automatically, before the conscious question forms. The standard isn't held. It's felt.

### Behavioral Profile

**How they experience the world**
Quality registers before anything else — in spaces, in work, in people. This is not snobbery; it is perception. The feeling of something being slightly wrong is almost physical.

**What they're genuinely good at**
Discernment that is structural rather than effortful — perceives quality and its absence automatically and accurately. Precision in evaluation that produces assessments others can actually rely on. Commitment to genuine excellence that, when applied, produces work of lasting quality.
