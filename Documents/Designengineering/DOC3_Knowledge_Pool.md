## PART 1 — Sourcing Protocol and Flags

Every source entry in this document carries one of three flags:

| Flag | Meaning | What you can do with it |
|---|---|---|
| **SOURCE-FROM** | Directly applicable to this key. Anchored in the canon. | Run the derivation question. Add result to Tier 2 inventory. |
| **VERIFY-ONLY** | Interpretive extension. Confirms Tier 1 content; shapes how you write, not what you claim. | Use as framing. Do not derive behavioral claims from it directly. |
| **EXCLUDE** | Not anchored in this document's sourcing standard. | Do not use regardless of general BaZi knowledge. |

**The derivation question method** — mandatory for every SOURCE-FROM entry:

> "What specific behavioral claim does this classical principle produce for this combination — beyond what Tier 2 psychology already covers?"

If the answer is "nothing new," proceed with Tier 2 only. That is a valid outcome. Classical sources are not required to add behavioral claims — they are required to be checked.

**Hard ceilings:**
- Universal tgPattern principles: max **2 behavioral claims** per SOURCE-FROM entry
- Stem-specific conditionals: max **3 behavioral claims** per SOURCE-FROM entry
- TG family principles: max **2 behavioral claims per polarity** per SOURCE-FROM entry

Never exceed these ceilings even if the source seems to offer more. Readings that pile on classical claims produce analytical text, not vivid portraits.

### 穷通宝鉴 — Climate Adjustment Framework

**What it covers:** 调候用神 — the seasonal override layer. How the month of birth (and therefore the seasonal temperature of the chart) modifies which elements are beneficial or harmful, independent of the 扶抑 (support/suppress) baseline.

**Core principle:** 论命惟以月令用神为主，然亦须配气候而互参之
*"In reading fate, the month branch useful god is primary, but one must also cross-reference the climate."*
Climate is a modifier on top of 扶抑 — not a replacement system.

**SOURCE-FROM — Metal stems (庚 and 辛):**
```
Principle A: 金逢火炼方成器
"Metal meeting the tempering of Fire becomes an instrument."
```
素材 (raw material) vs. 成器 (an instrument shaped for a specific purpose). Fire does not add to the precision — it *specifies* what the precision is an instrument for. 通明达理 (clear-minded and communicable) is about quality of expression; 成器 is about identity of purpose. Two distinct claims.

Derivation A: The archetype is 素材 — fully formed, genuinely capable, unspecified as to purpose. Fire converts 素材 to 成器 by specifying, not by adding.

```
Principle B: 金逢火炼方显锋芒
"Metal meeting the tempering of Fire reveals its sharp edge."
```
显 (reveals) is the operative word — not creates. The edge existed before Fire arrived. Fire makes it visible and purposefully deployed.

Derivation B: Relief is not "becoming capable" or "being recognised." It is the precision finding a target it fully believes in — one it didn't have to generate from within itself alone.

**Tier 2 inventory for Metal stems:**
1. The archetype is 素材 — formed, capable, unspecified. Fire converts by specifying, not adding. → *[p2 relief clause, twoAM]*
2. The edge was already real. Fire changes manifest specificity, not the capability. → *[teaser, landscape]*
3. The structural tension: "I am formed and complete and have not yet been specified toward a purpose that fully deserves this." → *[twoAM — more precise than SDT alone]*

Max: **3 behavioral claims for Metal stems.** Hard ceiling.

"甲 Wood welcomes 庚 Metal's shaping; then it becomes useful timber."

原木 (raw growth) vs. 成材 (timber shaped into useful form). The shaping force doesn't stop the growth — it defines what the growth consolidates into.

Derivation: 成材 adds what SDT alone cannot produce — the Oak's growth without a shaping force *spreads* rather than *consolidates*. Not "lacks direction" — the direction is always present. What is absent is the force that converts reaching into something with defined form.

#### `seasonalCalibration` field — definition and sourcing

**Internal field name:** `seasonalCalibration`
**Source system:** 调候用神 from 穷通宝鉴 — the *seasonal override layer*, distinct from 病药用神 (catalyst/remedy) sourced from 神峰通考.

These two systems answer different questions and frequently point to different elements:
- **病药用神** (catalyst/remedy) — answers: *"What does this DM need structurally to function at its best?"* Derived from 扶抑 analysis (strengthen/suppress balance).
- **调候用神** (seasonal calibration) — answers: *"What does this chart need to not be frozen or burnt by its seasonal temperature?"* Derived from month branch climate analysis in 穷通宝鉴.

They must be kept as separate fields. Collapsing them into the catalyst field produces incorrect readings when the elements diverge.

**Tier:** Pro. Surfaces in reading text under a per-element poetic user-facing label.

**User-facing label convention (internal → user-facing):**
| Element | Internal name | User-facing label (proposed) |
|---|---|---|
| Metal (金) | `seasonalCalibration` | The Forging Season |
| Wood (木) | `seasonalCalibration` | The Growing Season |
| Fire (火) | `seasonalCalibration` | The Tempering Season |
| Earth (土) | `seasonalCalibration` | The Settling Season |
| Water (水) | `seasonalCalibration` | The Still Season |

*Labels are proposals — confirm during content generation for each element.*

**What this field contains:** Not a single catalyst element, but a contextual reading: *given the seasonal temperature of this chart (month branch), what element, season, or environmental condition brings the structure into its most workable state.* Framed in second person. Experiential, not prescriptive. Draws from the Tier 2 inventory above for each stem.

### 神峰通考 (张神峰) — Deficiency/Remedy Theory

**What it covers:** 病药说 — the principle that structural distinction arises from structural deficiency, and the chart's value is revealed when the remedy element arrives. Also contains a sustained critique of mechanical application of classical rules: the principle matters; the formula is secondary.

**Core principle:**
```
有病方为贵，无病不是奇
"Only when there is deficiency is there distinction; without deficiency there is nothing to be specified toward."
```

The 病 (deficiency/imbalance) is not a flaw — it is the structural source of distinctiveness. The 药 (remedy element) does not "fix" the person. It specifies what the archetype becomes when the missing element arrives. Without the 病, the chart has no specific register — it is complete but unspecified.

**SOURCE-FROM — Universal (applies across all stems and patterns):**

The catalyst element in Elementum readings is structurally the 药 for the chart's 病. The reading implication:

Derivation A: The archetype is fully formed without the catalyst — not incomplete, not lesser. The 药 specifies; it does not rescue. The precision of 庚 Metal has no named purpose until Fire specifies what instrument it becomes. The Ocean's depth (壬) has no form until Earth contains it. The capability was already real.

Derivation B: The twoAM register for every archetype is the condition of being formed and complete and unspecified. Not lacking capability — lacking the element that converts capability into a specific direction fully believed in. The correct framing is waiting, not broken.

**Tier 2 inventory — universal application:**
1. The catalyst is 药 for a structural 病 — specification, not correction. The archetype is formed; the catalyst reveals what it was all along. → *[teaser, p2 relief clause — universal]*
2. Without the catalyst, the archetype is complete and unspecified — not broken, not waiting to be fixed. The twoAM state is being fully formed and not yet made specific toward what deserves this. → *[twoAM — universal across all stems]*

Max: **2 behavioral claims.** Universal ceiling.

**Critical writing rule:** Do not frame the catalyst as something the person "needs" in the emotional or compensatory sense. The 药 is not a dependency; it is a structural specifier. The reading must hold both poles simultaneously: fully capable without it, and made distinct by it. This is the difference between "you need this element" (wrong register) and "this element reveals what was already true" (correct register).

### 千里命稿 (韦千里) — Life Stage Mapping

**What it covers:** 宫位论法 — the mapping of the four pillars to developmental life stages. Each pillar contains energies that manifest most visibly in a corresponding period of the person's life.

**Status: VERIFY-ONLY.** 千里命稿 is a 20th-century practical application text, not primary classical canon. The pillar-to-stage mapping is interpretive convention derived from classical principles, not directly sourced from the canonical five texts. Use as framing for *when* certain dynamics manifest; do not derive behavioral claims from the mapping itself.

| Pillar | Life domain | Approximate age range | Reading implication |
|---|---|---|---|
| 年柱 (Year) | Family background, ancestral pattern, childhood environment | 1–16 | Energies here shaped the formative conditions |
| 月柱 (Month) | Career foundation, social/work context, parent environment | 17–32 | Also the seasonal authority column — dominant in chart logic |
| 日柱 (Day) | Self (Day Master stem), partner/relationship (Day Branch) | 33–48 | The DM's core expression; the Day Branch as relational texture |
| 时柱 (Hour) | Legacy, output, later-life chapter, what is left behind | 49+ | Output themes that consolidate in later life |

**Reading implication (VERIFY-ONLY):** When a chart's dominant elemental energy is heavily weighted in a specific pillar, its behavioral expression tends to manifest most visibly in the corresponding life stage. A Month-pillar-dominant chart shows its structural pattern most intensely in the career-formation period. A Hour-pillar-dominant chart has output and legacy themes that emerge late rather than early.

Use this framing to add temporal precision to the `landscape` and `twoAM` fields when the chart's energy concentration in a specific pillar is notable. Do not derive behavioral claims from the mapping — use it to inform the period of life the reading's language most naturally speaks to.

**⚠ Conditionality:** Apply only when pillar concentration is marked (dominant energy heavily weighted in one pillar rather than distributed across all four). Do not apply the stage mapping as a universal framing device — most charts have distributed energies, and the mapping adds nothing when distribution is even.

### §3.1 — Stem Profiles (Level 1: Cognitive/Relational Orientation)

The stem describes the person's default mode of engaging with reality. Not a trait (how much of something) but an **orientation** (how the person processes and responds). Jung's cognitive functions are the most precise analog because they describe *how* people engage — not quantities of personality, but qualities of engagement.

**Important caveat:** BaZi yin/yang polarity within a stem pair (甲/乙, 庚/辛, etc.) is NOT introversion/extraversion in the Jungian sense. Polarity describes the mode of the element's expression — outward/structural vs. inward/adaptive — which correlates with E/I but is not identical to it.

---

#### 甲 Yang Wood — The Oak

- **Primary Jungian analog:** Extraverted Intuition (Ne) + Extraverted Thinking (Te). Generates possibilities and reaches toward them — the next stage is always visible before the current one has consolidated.
- **Big Five signature:** High Openness/Intellect + High Assertiveness facet of Extraversion + variable Industriousness (the challenge is consolidation, not initiation)
- **DeYoung facet:** Strong on Intellect (speculative, idea-generative); challenge at Orderliness (low natural impulse toward completion)
- **Key nuance:** The defining struggle is not capability — it is **consolidation vs. extension**. The vision outruns the foundations. This is the structural cost of the Oak's greatest strength.
- **Attachment signature:** Secure-leaning with over-extension risk — commits deeply then outgrows the commitment before fully rooting.

#### 乙 Yin Wood — The Vine

- **Primary Jungian analog:** Introverted Intuition (Ni). Perceives the underlying structure, the route, the destination — often before it can explain its reasoning.
- **Big Five signature:** High Openness + moderate-high Agreeableness/Compassion + moderate Introversion tendency
- **Key nuance:** Not passive — *strategically adaptive*. Proactive in destination, reactive in path. The Oak grows because it cannot stop; the Vine navigates because it always knows where it's going.
- **Attachment signature:** Anxious-avoidant — adapts to environment to survive, producing both extraordinary sensitivity and chameleon risk.

#### 丙 Yang Fire — The Sun

- **Primary Jungian analog:** Extraverted Feeling (Fe). The warmth is structurally outward — it shapes the emotional climate of whatever space it occupies, not as a performance but as a property of its presence.
- **Big Five signature:** High Enthusiasm facet of Extraversion + High Compassion/Agreeableness + moderate Neuroticism (the accumulation cost of structural warmth)
- **Key nuance:** SDT's **relatedness need** is highly activated. Wellbeing is bound up with connection quality. The warmth is real but can feel automatic rather than chosen to the person themselves — the giving that doesn't feel like giving.
- **Attachment signature:** Secure with abandonment sensitivity — the giving is genuine; the depletion is real when unrecognised.

#### 丁 Yin Fire — The Candle

- **Primary Jungian analog:** Introverted Feeling (Fi). The attention is focused by deep inner conviction — illuminates what it has *chosen* to illuminate, fully and completely.
- **Big Five signature:** Lower Extraversion (selective) + High Conscientiousness/Orderliness and Responsibility facets + moderate Neuroticism potential
- **Key nuance:** **Gregarious warmth** (Fe — giving broadly) vs. **targeted intimacy** (Fi — giving deeply to what is chosen). The Candle is not a smaller Sun — it is a fundamentally different mode of caring. SDT's **autonomy need** is extremely high — the illumination only works when self-directed.
- **Attachment signature:** Secure in deep bonds, avoidant with surface relationships — focused attention creates intensity most cannot sustain.

#### 戊 Yang Earth — The Mountain

- **Primary Jungian analog:** Introverted Sensation (Si) in its most stable expression — maintains the internal framework of what works, what has proven stable, what can be depended on.
- **Big Five signature:** High Conscientiousness across both facets + Low Neuroticism + moderate Extraversion
- **Key nuance:** **Structural reliability** vs. effortful Conscientiousness. The Mountain doesn't try to be reliable; reliability is the default state. Bowlby's secure base provision — provides psychological security without requiring acknowledgment. Shadow: stability so taken for granted that its own need for support goes systematically unrecognised.
- **Attachment signature:** Secure provider with stagnation risk — the stability that holds everything can hold itself in place.

#### 己 Yin Earth — The Field

- **Primary Jungian analog:** Introverted Feeling (Fi) in its most relational expression — cares specifically, not broadly. Nourishment is directed at what the specific person or situation actually needs.
- **Big Five signature:** High Agreeableness (both Compassion and Politeness facets) + moderate Conscientiousness + variable Extraversion
- **Key nuance:** The Field is misread as simply "agreeable." The defining feature is **developmental investment** — creating conditions for growth. Maps to parental investment theory and mentorship quality research, not simple social cooperation.
- **Attachment signature:** Anxious-preoccupied — high caregiving activation. Distress at inability to nourish is genuine. When caregiving is unreciprocated over time, the Field depletes invisibly.

#### 庚 Yang Metal — The Blade

- **Primary Jungian analog:** Introverted Thinking (Ti). The evaluation operates through an internal logical framework that cannot be switched off — runs before social consideration, before emotional processing, before the decision to engage.
- **Big Five signature:** High Conscientiousness/Industriousness + Low Agreeableness/Politeness + lower Neuroticism + moderate Introversion tendency
- **Key nuance:** Not **judgment** (which implies choice) but **evaluation** (which is structural). Research on need for cognition and evaluative orientation: some people process evaluatively as a default mode; others as an active choice. 庚 is the former. Analysis is *conclusion-seeking* not exploration-seeking.
- **Attachment signature:** Dismissive-avoidant — self-sufficient, low dependency. The evaluative default reads as distance by others. The care is real; it arrives through the same evaluative channel as everything else.

#### 辛 Yin Metal — The Jewel

- **Primary Jungian analog:** Introverted Sensation (Si) in its most refined expression + Introverted Feeling (Fi) for aesthetic valuation. Discernment is both perceptual (registers quality as others register temperature) and evaluative (assigns internal value).
- **Big Five signature:** High Openness/Aesthetics facet (DeYoung: Aesthetics = quality-seeking; Intellect = truth-seeking — 辛 is on the Aesthetics side) + High Conscientiousness/Orderliness + moderate-low Extraversion
- **Key nuance:** 庚 evaluates *logically* (is this accurate?); 辛 evaluates *aesthetically* (is this genuinely excellent?). Research on perfectionism: healthy perfectionism (standards that motivate quality) vs. maladaptive perfectionism (standards as chronic dissatisfaction). 辛 sits precisely on this axis.
- **Attachment signature:** Fearful-avoidant — deep relational capacity with high threshold. The discernment that identifies excellence also identifies what doesn't meet it.

#### 壬 Yang Water — The Ocean

- **Primary Jungian analog:** Introverted Thinking (Ti) at vast scale + Introverted Intuition (Ni) generating systemic insight. Processes comprehensively, holding multiple frameworks simultaneously.
- **Big Five signature:** High Openness/Intellect + lower Extraversion Enthusiasm + high Conscientiousness/Industriousness + variable Neuroticism
- **Key nuance:** Defining feature is **depth + withholding**. Introverted high-openness types process far more than they surface. SDT: competence and autonomy needs extremely high — requires contexts that honour the full depth. When exchange quality is insufficient, the Ocean retreats rather than simplifying.
- **Attachment signature:** Dismissive-avoidant — the depth that perceives everything can produce emotional distance. Holding more internally than gets shown is constitutional, not a choice.
- **Key distinction from 癸:** The Ocean holds depth actively (knows more, retains more, analyses extensively); the Rain senses depth passively (perceives what is true without reasoning toward it).

#### 癸 Yin Water — The Rain

- **Primary Jungian analog:** Introverted Intuition (Ni) + Introverted Feeling (Fi). Perceives at depth and cares specifically — the intuition is immediate and uncanny, the care goes where most needed.
- **Big Five signature:** High Neuroticism/Withdrawal facet (sensitivity is structurally high — the permeability that makes perception possible) + High Agreeableness/Compassion + lower Extraversion
- **Key nuance:** The Rain's Neuroticism is not primarily anxious — it is *perceptual*. Best framed via Elaine Aron's **Highly Sensitive Person (HSP)** research: deeper cognitive processing, greater sensitivity to subtleties, stronger emotional reactivity — simultaneously the source of the greatest gift (perceiving what is true before it is spoken) and the characteristic burden (difficulty maintaining the distinction between perception and absorption).
- **Attachment signature:** Anxious-preoccupied — permeability means proximity to others is not emotionally neutral. The Rain absorbs the emotional state of those around it.

### §3.2 — Band Profiles (Level 2: Intensity and Regulation)

The band describes how fully and unconditionally the stem's mechanism expresses. Three fundamentally different psychological architectures — not a volume dial.

#### Concentrated — Self-Generating Mode

- **Trait activation theory (Tett & Guterman, 2003):** Traits are activated by situational cues. The concentrated band means the trait activates *regardless of situational cues* — it runs because it runs, not because the situation calls for it.
- **Integrative complexity (Tetlock, 1983):** The concentrated person is not less capable of complexity — they have less *internal counterforce* producing it spontaneously. Potent in their distinctive domain; more brittle at the boundaries.
- **Regulatory focus theory (Higgins, 1997):** Concentrated charts tend toward strong promotion OR prevention focus (depending on the element) with high intensity. The focus is clear, consistent, and hard to redirect.
- **Reading implication:** A concentrated 庚 is not *more Metal* than a balanced 庚 — they are the *same* Metal with less internal correction. The self-referencing loop has no natural interrupt.

#### Balanced — Resonant Mode

- **Jung's individuation:** The balanced band maps to individuation's achievement — not the absence of the dominant function, but its integration with opposing functions. A fundamentally different structural condition, not a less vivid version of concentrated.
- **SDT's integrated regulation:** The balanced person has moved from automatic expression of their nature to genuinely autonomous engagement with it — the character is directing itself rather than running as an automatic process.
- **Research implication:** Highest *adaptive performance* across diverse contexts — not peak performance in a single domain (that belongs to concentrated charts) but the most consistently reliable performance across varying conditions.

#### Open — Receptive Mode

- **Person-environment fit theory (Holland, 1985):** Environment quality is not merely relevant but *categorical* — the right conditions produce dramatically different results than the wrong conditions.
- **Aron's Highly Sensitive Person (HSP):** The open band often has a sensitivity component — the person's output is more attuned to contextual quality. The right conditions can produce extraordinary results; the wrong conditions produce much more pronounced difficulty than for less context-sensitive types.
- **SDT's three basic needs:** The open band most strongly benefits from genuine autonomy support, competence feedback, and relatedness quality. When these are satisfied, intrinsic motivation operates at full capacity; when thwarted, underperformance is visible.
- **Reading implication:** Never frame the open band as a weaker version of concentrated. The range is wider at both ends — higher ceiling, lower floor.

### §3.3 — tgPattern Profiles (Level 3: Structural Force)

The tgPattern describes the dominant external force shaping the DM's expression. The polarity split within Output (食神/伤官) and Authority (正官/七杀) is resolved at Layer 2 — the profiles below describe the structural family condition.

#### pure — Self-Element Dominant

- **Jung's one-sidedness (Einseitigkeit):** When the dominant function runs without adequate development of its opposite, the person becomes increasingly identified with one pole. Simultaneously the source of greatest competence and greatest rigidity. The pure pattern is structural one-sidedness.
- **Markus (1977) self-schema consolidation:** A highly consolidated self-schema produces consistent identity at the cost of permeability to new information that challenges the schema's core. Conclusions settle into facts; the schema defends itself efficiently against discrepant input.
- **Key nuance:** Gift and shadow are *structurally identical* — concentrated without modulation is potent AND brittle simultaneously.

#### rooted — Resource/Seal Dominant

- **Bowlby (1969) secure base provision:** The rooted pattern produces a character that has been constitutionally supported. People who have never felt the full existential urgency of being without support operate differently from those who have.
- **SDT's externally supported autonomy:** The well the person draws from is outside themselves — stable enough to feel internal, but external in origin.
- **Key nuance:** NOT simply "supported people are better off." The critical insight: sudden loss of the secure base produces disproportionate disorientation because the internal resources for self-soothing were never forced to fully develop.

#### flowing — Output Element Dominant

- **SDT's intrinsic motivation (Ryan & Deci, 1985):** Doing because doing is its own reward. The output emerges from inherent interest rather than external pressure or reward.
- **Csikszentmihalyi (1990) flow states:** Deep, effortless engagement where challenge and skill are matched without deliberate calibration.
- **Key nuance:** Shadow is precisely the effortlessness — the self doesn't monitor whether output is sustainable because it never feels like effort. Polarity split resolved in Layer 2: 食神 = non-assertive, effortless; 伤官 = assertive into resistance.

#### forging — Wealth Element Dominant

- **McClelland's Achievement Motivation (nAch, 1961):** Strong orientation toward mastery and directing outcomes — specifically about personal mastery, not following rules (Conscientiousness) or seeking recognition (Extraversion).
- **SDT's competence need:** Wellbeing is bound up with feeling effective — with being able to direct what needs to be directed.
- **Key nuance:** NOT just Conscientiousness. Conscientiousness measures self-regulation; the forging pattern measures **outward-directed control and acquisition**. Shadow: self-depletion when the directing self loses access to the resources it has been directing.

#### tested — Authority Element Dominant

- **Tyler (2006) legitimacy theory:** Normative compliance (I follow the rules because the framework is legitimate and I have chosen to accept it) vs. instrumental compliance (I have to). Framework-mediated vs. unmediated.
- **PTG research (Tedeschi & Calhoun, 1996):** The same adversarial conditions devastate some people and produce remarkable growth in others. The *nature* of the conditions (uncontrolled, high-intensity) is specifically associated with PTG.
- **Key nuance:** Polarity split is the most important distinction in the system. 正官 (cross-polarity): framework-mediated, grants recognition when quality is real, produces integrated regulation. 七杀 (same-polarity): unmediated, does not grant permission, does not moderate itself, produces either exceptional forged character or significant damage. Not different intensities — categorically different psychological processes. Resolved in Layer 2.

### §3.4 — Elemental Processing Modes (Layer 2: The Field Each DM Operates Within)

When a non-DM element dominates the chart, it is not just a symbolic presence — it creates a psychological *field* that the DM operates within. These five descriptions ground the Layer 2 `works` angle.

#### Metal as Psychological Field

**Field quality:** Evaluation before engagement. Precision as the primary cognitive event. Excellence perceived before it's reasoned toward.

**Mechanism:** DeYoung's Intellect + Conscientiousness/Orderliness. The Metal field produces evaluation-first processing. When Metal is the dominant force in a chart, the DM's mechanism is surrounded by and filtered through this evaluative medium.

**Interaction implications:** Metal amplifies evaluation for Metal DMs (比肩/劫财 self-reference); sets the standard for Wood DMs (正官/七杀 — the cutting force that tests or judges); becomes material for Fire DMs (偏财/正财 — warmth applied to precision); is naturally produced by Earth DMs (食神/伤官 — stability generating precision); and sustains depth for Water DMs (偏印/正印 — precision as the source that keeps perceptual intelligence sharp).

#### Wood as Psychological Field

**Field quality:** Developmental momentum. Reach toward growth and becoming. The directional force that converts potential into form.

**Mechanism:** Extraverted Intuition (Ne) + achievement motivation toward developmental outcomes. The Wood field produces growth-oriented processing and creates a climate of developmental possibility.

**Interaction implications:** Wood amplifies reach for Wood DMs (比肩/劫财 — generosity compounding); nourishes warmth for Fire DMs (偏印/正印 — the fuel); presses against stability for Earth DMs (七杀/正官 — roots break stone); becomes material for Metal DMs (偏财/正财 — living material the precision directs); and is generated by Water DMs (食神/伤官 — depth flowing outward as developmental energy).

#### Fire as Psychological Field

**Field quality:** Warmth as climate. Relational presence. Illumination that changes what it touches without necessarily being acknowledged.

**Mechanism:** Extraverted Feeling (Fe) + SDT's relatedness need. The Fire field produces warmth-first processing and creates a relational climate around whatever it surrounds.

**Interaction implications:** Fire amplifies presence for Fire DMs (比肩/劫财 — warmth compounding); activates stability for Earth DMs (偏印/正印 — the warming element); presses on precision for Metal DMs (七杀/正官 — the forge); is generated by Wood DMs (食神/伤官 — reach flowing into warmth); and becomes material for Water DMs (偏财/正财 — depth giving warmth form).

#### Earth as Psychological Field

**Field quality:** Stability as ground. Load-bearing holding. Accumulation and the reliability of what endures.

**Mechanism:** Introverted Sensation (Si) + high Conscientiousness + structural reliability (Bowlby's secure base at environmental level). The Earth field produces stability-first processing.

**Interaction implications:** Earth amplifies stability for Earth DMs (比肩/劫财 — holding force deepening); produces precision for Metal DMs (食神/伤官 — stability as natural source of precision); contains depth for Water DMs (七杀/正官 — dam that shapes or blocks); becomes material for Wood DMs (偏财/正财 — stable ground that reach can direct); and is generated by Fire DMs (偏印/正印 — warmth activating stability).

#### Water as Psychological Field

**Field quality:** Depth of perception. Intelligence beneath the surface. Flowing form that holds more than it shows.

**Mechanism:** Introverted Intuition (Ni) + Introverted Thinking (Ti). HSP depth processing. The Water field produces depth-first processing and creates a climate of perceptual intelligence.

**Interaction implications:** Water amplifies depth for Water DMs (比肩/劫财 — intelligence compounding without form); is generated by Metal DMs (食神/伤官 — precision flowing into perceptual intelligence); becomes material for Earth DMs (偏财/正财 — depth that stability directs); nourishes reach for Wood DMs (偏印/正印 — depth sustaining developmental instinct); and presses on warmth for Fire DMs (七杀/正官 — the extinguishing force).

### §3.5 — Ten God Psychological Profiles (Layer 2: Specific TG Mechanisms)

Each profile gives the specific psychological mechanism that illuminates this TG relationship. These are the Tier 1 inputs for `buildAnglesPrompt()`. The polarity pair distinction must drive structural differences in generated content.

#### 比肩 — The Mirror (same-polarity companion)

- **Primary:** Markus (1977) self-schema consolidation — highly consolidated self-schemas become progressively resistant to discrepant input, not through hostility but through efficient processing that routes inconsistent information as irrelevant.
- **Secondary:** Dismissive-avoidant attachment (Bartholomew & Horowitz, 1991) — high self-model positivity, low other-model dependency.
- **Tertiary:** Wiggins (1979/1991) interpersonal circumplex — high Agency, assured-dominant position. Interpersonal interactions mediated through quality-matching rather than relatedness-seeking.
- **Key nuance:** 比肩 self-amplification is NOT narcissistic self-promotion (seeking external validation). It is structural self-consistency — the mechanism runs, confirms itself, and the confirmation is the output. The loneliness produced is not from being alone; it is from a complete system that requires extraordinary peers to enter.

#### 劫财 — The Rival (cross-polarity companion)

- **Primary:** Festinger (1954) social comparison theory — when downward or upward comparison is unavailable, lateral comparison with similar others becomes primary.
- **Secondary:** Kiesler (1983) complementarity principle — similar-status interactions produce comparison orientation rather than role differentiation.
- **Key nuance:** 劫财 rivalry is not hostile. It is the structural consequence of a permanent reference point that is close enough to be meaningful and different enough to be competitive. The most important relationships for 劫财 charts are with people like them — both the most useful and the most difficult to surrender to.

#### 食神 — The Flow (same-polarity output)

- **Primary:** Deci & Ryan (1985) SDT intrinsic motivation — behavior that is "inherently interesting and enjoyable" without external pressure.
- **Secondary:** Csikszentmihalyi (1990) flow states — deep, effortless engagement where challenge and skill match without deliberate calibration.
- **Key nuance:** The shadow is precisely the effortlessness. Because output doesn't feel like effort from the inside, depletion accumulates invisibly. Research on intrinsic motivation documents this: over-extension into what feels natural is not lazy — it is the specific risk of the person for whom doing never registered as work.

#### 伤官 — The Edge (cross-polarity output)

- **Primary:** Forster & Higgins (1996) reactive creativity — creativity that emerges in response to constraints is structurally different from creativity in comfortable conditions. The 伤官 output is *constituted* by the friction it meets.
- **Secondary:** SDT's autonomy need under threat (Ryan & Deci, 2000) — when autonomy is thwarted, high-autonomy individuals push back. The 伤官 output is in structural tension with authority not from willful rebellion but because the output genuinely exceeds the existing framework.
- **Tertiary:** Innovation personality research (Patterson et al., 2009; Furnham et al.) — High Openness + Low Agreeableness/Politeness profile.
- **Key nuance:** 食神 is generous (gives because giving is structural); 伤官 is assertive into resistance (the brilliance is partially constituted by the friction). The shadow of 食神 is over-extension; the shadow of 伤官 is self-destruction when the output cannot find an adequate container.

#### 偏财 — The Field (same-polarity wealth)

- **Primary:** McClelland's nAch (1961) in diffuse expression — achievement motivation distributed across many domains rather than concentrated in one.
- **Secondary:** SDT's competence need in its wide form — effectiveness across diverse contexts rather than in a specific domain.
- **Key nuance:** What is controlled broadly is never fully possessed, and what is never fully possessed can be lost without the person fully registering what they had. The gift is range and natural abundance; the shadow is breadth without the consolidation that allows what is touched to become specifically yours.

#### 正财 — The Harvest (cross-polarity wealth)

- **Primary:** McClelland's nAch (1961) in specific expression + SDT's competence need focused — high achievement motivation directed at a particular domain: methodical, disciplined, earned acquisition.
- **Secondary:** DeYoung's Conscientiousness facets (2010) — Orderliness + Industriousness applied outward. The 正财 person organises not themselves but the resources around them.
- **Key nuance:** The specific shadow is what the evaluative apparatus does to what it controls over time. The precision applied to acquired material doesn't know when to stop asking whether what it holds is worthy of the standard.

#### 七杀 — The Trial (same-polarity authority)

- **Primary:** Tedeschi & Calhoun (1996) Post-Traumatic Growth — the same adversarial conditions that devastate some people produce remarkable growth in others. Same structural condition, categorically different outcomes. This is a genuine bifurcation, not a developmental spectrum.
- **Secondary:** Kobasa (1979) hardiness — three components buffering against uncontrolled stress: commitment (to goals and self), control (perceived capacity to influence), and challenge (viewing pressure as opportunity). Whether all three were available determines which side of the bifurcation the character lands on.
- **Tertiary:** McAdams et al. (2001) narrative identity — redemption sequences (bad → good) vs. contamination sequences (good → bad). High-resource 七杀 charts construct redemption sequences; depleted ones construct contamination sequences.
- **Key nuance:** 七杀 does not grant permission. Does not moderate itself. Does not care whether you survive the encounter. What this builds — when the chart has sufficient resources — is character that could only have come from being forged, not developed. What others carry as virtue, this person carries as the residue of surviving something that didn't grant them the option to fail gracefully.

#### 正官 — The Standard (cross-polarity authority)

- **Primary:** Tyler (2006) legitimacy theory — normative compliance (I follow because the framework is legitimate and I have endorsed it) vs. instrumental compliance (I follow because I have to). The 正官 chart describes normative compliance at its most structurally embedded.
- **Secondary:** SDT's identified regulation (Ryan & Deci, 2000) — the highest form of extrinsic motivation, where behavior aligns with identity and values even though it originated outside the self.
- **Tertiary:** Bowlby (1969) secure base in authority — 正官 is the DM's external secure base: the structure that enables confident exploration within its domain, whose loss creates disproportionate disorientation.
- **Key nuance:** 正官 vs. 七杀 is not "nice pressure vs. harsh pressure." It is permission-granting vs. non-permission-granting; framework-mediated vs. unmediated; character refined within a structure vs. character forged despite the absence of one. Different personality architectures.

#### 偏印 — The Well (same-polarity resource)

- **Primary:** Bowlby (1969) + Ainsworth (1978) secure-base dependency. The 偏印 chart draws continuously from a source that sustains and deepens without redirecting — consolidates what is already present without proposing new directions.
- **Secondary:** SDT's relatedness need in its receptive form — being nourished by the source rather than actively seeking connection. The backing is felt as ground rather than relationship.
- **Key nuance:** 偏印 nourishment is deepening without opening. The person may be extraordinarily deep in their domain because the sustaining source keeps deepening what's already there — and may be extraordinarily dependent on conditions because those conditions never required them to build the internal source.

#### 正印 — The Root (cross-polarity resource)

- **Primary:** Bowlby (1969) secure base in its most developmental form — the base that enables exploration by providing both support and direction. The backing includes a direction, not only sustenance.
- **Secondary:** SDT's need satisfaction through developmental relationship — relatedness and autonomy simultaneously met by the same source.
- **Key nuance:** 正印 nourishment is opening as well as sustaining. Cross-polarity means the resource comes from a different register and therefore gestures toward what is not yet present. The shadow: the direction given with the backing can become the only direction the DM knows — reach that learned to grow only where the nourishment pointed.

## PART 5 — Expansion Protocol

### When to expand

Add a new source when:
1. The existing Tier 1 + Tier 2 pool cannot produce the specific behavioral claim that differentiates a yin/yang TG pair counterpart
2. A new stem combination exposes a gap not covered by the current stem profiles
3. A specific interaction (`domEl × tenGod`) consistently produces flat or generic angles that fail the quality gate

Do not add sources to make readings "richer" in general. Sources are added only to close specific gaps.

### How to evaluate a candidate source

| Criterion | Required? | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Peer-reviewed or established canon | Required for Tier 2 | Commentary-register classical texts qualify if concept-reliable across sources |
| Produces a specific behavioral claim | Required | "Interesting theory" is not enough — the derivation question must yield something specific |
| Does not duplicate existing Tier 1/2 inventory | Required | Check both classical and psychological inventories before adding |
| Can be paired with a derivation question | Required | Source without derivation question is unusable |

### How to add a source

1. Identify which tier it belongs to (Classical → §2; Psychology → §3; Resonance → §4)
2. Assign the flag: SOURCE-FROM / VERIFY-ONLY / EXCLUDE
3. Write the derivation question alongside the source — always, without exception
4. Apply the hard ceiling: max 2–3 behavioral claims per entry
5. Note any conditionality (stem-specific, pattern-specific, polarity-specific)
6. Add to the version history with date and brief description

### Hard constraints

- Never use: general BaZi commentary websites, astrology sources as Tier 2, non-peer-reviewed psychological claims as Tier 2
- Never exceed the ceiling (2–3 behavioral claims per source entry) even if the source seems to offer more
- Never replace existing SOURCE-FROM material — only supplement gaps
- All user-facing output must use the three-register vocabulary framework regardless of which sources are used

### §7.1 — Branch Relationships: Element Score Modifiers

Branch relationships modify effective element scores beyond simple hidden-stem weighting. The engine must account for these when computing elemental balances. These are the structural interactions that can substantially shift which element scores as dominant — and therefore which Layer 2/3 key a chart receives.

**Six Clashes (六冲):** Directly opposing branches clash — weakens effective element contribution of both branches involved. Approximate effect: 20–30% reduction in hidden-stem weight for each clashing branch.

Clash pairs: 子午 · 丑未 · 寅申 · 卯酉 · 辰戌 · 巳亥

Accuracy check: When a clash pair appears across pillars (especially Year-Day or Month-Hour), neither branch should be counted at full hidden-stem weight.

**Three Harmony Groups (三合局):** All three branches required for full reinforcement. Missing one = half-combination, approx. 50% of full reinforcement.

Groups: 申子辰 (Water) · 亥卯未 (Wood) · 寅午戌 (Fire) · 巳酉丑 (Metal)

Accuracy check: Full Three Harmony significantly boosts that element's effective score. If the engine does not adjust for branch combinations, element scores will be understated for strongly-grouped charts.

**Three Direction Groups (三会局):** Stronger than Three Harmony — directional (seasonal) grouping. All three required; no partial credit.

Groups: 寅卯辰 (Wood, Spring) · 巳午未 (Fire, Summer) · 申酉戌 (Metal, Autumn) · 亥子丑 (Water, Winter)

**Six Harmonies (六合):** Paired branch combinations — softer elemental modification.

Pairs: 子丑 (Earth) · 寅亥 (Wood) · 卯戌 (Fire) · 辰酉 (Metal) · 巳申 (Water) · 午未 (Fire)

**Stem Combinations (天干五合):** When two adjacent stems combine, their individual elemental force is partially bound. Transformation into the stated element requires: seasonal authority + adjacent stems + supporting branch — all three conditions. Without transformation, treat as occupied/bound: reduced contribution, not transformed.

Combinations: 甲己 (Earth) · 乙庚 (Metal) · 丙辛 (Water) · 丁壬 (Wood) · 戊癸 (Fire)

Accuracy check: Stem combinations are a common source of miscalculation. Do not assume transformation — verify all three conditions before applying transformation scoring.
